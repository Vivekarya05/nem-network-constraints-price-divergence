Analyst Story

The project story in market reasoning language: what happened, why it matters, and what the analysis concluded.

1. Market Symptom

NSW1 recorded 53 price spike intervals above $300/MWh during February 2026. VIC1 did not breach the selected spike threshold.

  • NSW1 became the primary high-price event region.
  • VIC1 remained important as the comparison region.
  • The project then asked whether network factors helped explain the difference.

2. Constraint Evidence

NSW1 spike intervals had higher average active constraint counts, but marginal value and violation evidence did not explain every spike alone.

  • Average active constraints increased during NSW1 spikes.
  • No violation intervals were observed during NSW1 spike periods.
  • Constraint evidence was relevant but not sufficient by itself.

3. Interconnector Evidence

Broader interconnector congestion-style signals appeared during most NSW1 spike intervals.

  • 49 of 53 NSW1 spike intervals had at least one congestion-style signal.
  • VIC1-NSW1 showed large maximum absolute flow and flow changes.
  • The strongest events were linked with network and price separation signals.

Regional Price Divergence

NSW1-VIC1 material divergence occurred in 307 intervals, while extreme divergence occurred in 60 intervals.

This showed that price separation was not constant, but was concentrated in important event windows.

Decision Intelligence

The project translated spike classifications into market situations, insights, recommendations, risks, and confidence levels.

This is the business-value layer: the analysis moves from detecting events to explaining what analysts should monitor.

Key Findings

Finding Analyst Interpretation
NSW1 Spikes NSW1 was the main high-price event region, with 53 intervals above $300/MWh.
Constraints Constraint activity increased during spikes, but marginal value and violation evidence did not explain all events alone.
Interconnectors Broader congestion-style signals were present during most NSW1 spike intervals.
Divergence NSW1-VIC1 price separation was material in concentrated periods, supporting the network-driven interpretation.
