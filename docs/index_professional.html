Analyst Findings

Market interpretation written for energy analysts, traders, and modellers.

Market Symptom

NSW1 recorded 53 spike intervals above $300/MWh, while VIC1 did not breach the selected spike threshold.

  • NSW1 became the main high-price event focus.
  • VIC1 remained critical for price separation analysis.

Constraint Evidence

NSW1 spike intervals had higher average active constraint counts, but violation and marginal value evidence did not explain every spike alone.

  • Constraint activity was relevant.
  • It was not a complete explanation by itself.

Network Evidence

Broader congestion-style signals appeared during most NSW1 spike intervals, and severe spikes aligned with price divergence.

  • Interconnector stress was material.
  • Regional separation supported the network interpretation.

Final Analyst Interpretation

A price spike is not automatically constraint-driven. This project showed that NSW1 spike intervals had elevated constraint activity, but the most severe price outcomes were more strongly associated with interconnector/congestion-style signals and NSW1-VIC1 price divergence.
